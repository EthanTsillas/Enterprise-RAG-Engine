Document Title: Illegal Unreported and Unregulated Fishing

Illegal, unreported, and unregulated (IUU) fishing accounts for an estimated 11 to 26 million metric tons of fish caught globally per year. The financial value of IUU fishing is estimated between 10 billion and 23.5 billion dollars annually. IUU fishing undermines legitimate fisheries management, making accurate stock assessments impossible. West Africa is among the most severely impacted regions, losing an estimated 2.3 billion dollars per year to illegal fishing. China operates the world's largest distant-water fishing fleet, with approximately 3,000 to 17,000 vessels operating internationally, some of which have been linked to IUU practices. The FAO Port State Measures Agreement, which entered into force in 2016, is the first binding international treaty specifically targeting IUU fishing. Satellite-based monitoring systems including Global Fishing Watch use AIS transponder data and machine learning to detect suspicious vessel behavior. Countries lacking strong monitoring systems lose an average of 21% of their reported catch to underreporting. IUU fishing disproportionately harms small island developing states that depend on fish for food security.
