Document Title: Global Overfishing Overview

Overfishing occurs when fish are caught faster than populations can reproduce. The United Nations Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO) estimates that 35.4% of global fish stocks are overfished as of 2023, up from 10% in 1974. Approximately 57.3% of stocks are fished at maximum sustainable levels. Only 7.3% of global stocks are underfished. World fish consumption has increased from 9 kilograms per person per year in 1961 to 20.5 kilograms per person per year in 2022. Global fishing fleets catch approximately 80 million metric tons of wild fish annually. The maximum sustainable yield for global fisheries is estimated at 80 to 100 million metric tons per year. Overfishing causes cascading ecosystem collapse, reducing biodiversity and destabilizing marine food webs. The global economic loss from overfishing is estimated at 83 billion dollars per year.
