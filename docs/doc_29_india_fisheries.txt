Document Title: India Fisheries Sector Overview

India is the third largest fish producing nation in the world, with total production of approximately 16.24 million metric tons in 2022 to 2023. India's marine fish catch from its 8,100-kilometer coastline is approximately 4 million metric tons per year. The Indian exclusive economic zone covers 2.02 million square kilometers, one of the largest in the world. Approximately 28 million people are employed in India's fisheries sector, with over 4 million active fishers. Kerala, Gujarat, Tamil Nadu, and Andhra Pradesh are the largest fish-producing states in India. The mechanized trawling fleet operating off India's coast has grown from fewer than 500 vessels in 1960 to over 70,000 vessels today, contributing to significant inshore stock depletion. India declared a 61-day annual fishing ban along its east coast and west coast during the monsoon season starting in 2015 to protect fish during peak spawning. Hilsa shad, a culturally important fish in West Bengal and Bangladesh, has seen dramatic population declines; the Ganga river hilsa catch declined from 600,000 metric tons in 1970 to under 10,000 metric tons by 2000. India's fish exports reached 8.09 billion dollars in 2022 to 2023, with shrimp accounting for approximately 65% of export value. The Blue Revolution scheme launched in 2015 has invested over 3.7 billion dollars to develop aquaculture and fisheries infrastructure in India.
