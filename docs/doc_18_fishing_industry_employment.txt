Document Title: Global Fishing Industry Employment and Livelihoods

Approximately 600 million people worldwide depend directly or indirectly on fisheries and aquaculture for their livelihoods. An estimated 58.5 million people are directly employed in fishing and aquaculture globally. Of those directly employed, approximately 84% work in small-scale artisanal fisheries. Women make up approximately 50% of the fisheries workforce globally when processing, distribution, and marketing are included. Small-scale fisheries account for approximately 40% of global fish catch by volume but represent over 90% of employment in the sector. Asia accounts for approximately 85% of the world's fishers, with China, Indonesia, India, and Vietnam being the largest employers. Fishing remains one of the most dangerous occupations globally, with an estimated 24,000 deaths at sea per year. The decline of fish stocks has driven economic migration away from traditional fishing communities in the Philippines, Senegal, and Peru. Fishing communities in New England lost an estimated 14,000 jobs following the collapse of groundfish stocks in the 1990s. Transitioning overfished communities to alternative livelihoods such as ecotourism, mariculture, and marine monitoring has shown mixed success depending on geographic and economic context.
