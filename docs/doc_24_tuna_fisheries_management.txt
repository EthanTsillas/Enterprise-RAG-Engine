Document Title: Tuna Fisheries Management and Regional Bodies

Five regional fisheries management organizations (RFMOs) manage tuna and tuna-like species globally: ICCAT for the Atlantic, IOTC for the Indian Ocean, WCPFC for the western and central Pacific, IATTC for the eastern Pacific, and CCSBT for southern bluefin tuna. The Western and Central Pacific Fisheries Commission (WCPFC) manages the world's largest tuna fishery, which produces approximately 2.8 million metric tons per year. Skipjack tuna is the most commercially important tuna species, accounting for approximately 67% of global tuna catch. Bigeye tuna is classified as vulnerable to extinction by the IUCN and is subject to catch limits from all five tuna RFMOs. Southern bluefin tuna, managed by CCSBT, declined by 90% from historical biomass before recovery measures implemented in 2011 began showing results. The Pacific Island nations of Kiribati, Federated States of Micronesia, and Palau earn the majority of their government revenue from tuna fishing license fees. Pole-and-line fishing and handline fishing produce the most selective tuna catch with minimal bycatch. The global canned tuna market is valued at approximately 10.7 billion dollars annually. Thailand, Ecuador, and Spain are the world's largest processors of canned tuna. Consumer campaigns including the Dolphin Safe label have successfully changed fishing practices in response to documented bycatch of dolphins in eastern Pacific tuna nets.
