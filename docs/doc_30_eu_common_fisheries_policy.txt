Document Title: European Union Common Fisheries Policy

The EU Common Fisheries Policy (CFP) is the framework governing fishing activities by EU member states in EU and international waters. The CFP was first established in 1983 and has undergone major reforms in 1992, 2002, and 2013. The 2013 CFP reform introduced a legally binding obligation to end overfishing and fish all stocks at maximum sustainable yield (MSY) levels by 2020, a target that was not achieved. As of 2022, 41% of assessed EU fish stocks are still overfished. The EU sets annual total allowable catches (TACs) for approximately 80 commercial fish stocks in EU waters. EU ministers have routinely set TACs above the scientific advice of the International Council for the Exploration of the Sea (ICES) due to political and economic pressure from member states. The EU fishing fleet consists of approximately 73,000 vessels employing around 150,000 fishers directly. Spain has the largest fishing fleet in the EU, followed by Greece and Italy. The EU imports approximately 65% of the seafood it consumes, making it the world's largest seafood importer. The EU's landing obligation, introduced in the 2013 reform, requires that all catches be landed rather than discarded at sea, but compliance has been inconsistent and difficult to enforce.
