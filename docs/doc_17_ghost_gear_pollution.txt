Document Title: Ghost Gear and Abandoned Fishing Equipment

Ghost gear refers to lost, abandoned, or discarded fishing equipment that continues to catch fish, marine mammals, seabirds, and sea turtles indiscriminately. An estimated 640,000 metric tons of ghost gear enters the ocean annually, representing approximately 10% of all marine plastic pollution. Derelict gillnets can continue fishing for months or years after abandonment, a phenomenon called ghost fishing. Ghost gear entanglement kills an estimated 136,000 seals, sea lions, and large whales annually. The Dungeness crab fishery on the US West Coast has documented over 10,000 active ghost traps on the seafloor at any given time. A single lost fishing net can entangle and kill marine life for decades before degrading. The Global Ghost Gear Initiative, launched in 2015, coordinates international efforts to reduce ghost gear through marking programs, retrieval operations, and improved gear design. Ghost gear retrieval operations in the Florida Keys National Marine Sanctuary have removed over 50 metric tons of derelict traps since 2004. Biodegradable escape panels in crab traps can reduce ghost fishing mortality by allowing crabs to escape if a trap is lost. The fishing industry generates approximately 5.7 times more ocean plastic waste per year than consumer packaging from fish products.
