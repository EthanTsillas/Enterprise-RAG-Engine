Document Title: Global Fishing Watch and AI Monitoring

Global Fishing Watch is a nonprofit organization that uses satellite data, machine learning, and cloud computing to monitor global fishing activity in near real time. Founded in 2016 by Google, Oceana, and SkyTruth, Global Fishing Watch makes its data publicly available at no cost. The platform tracks over 60,000 fishing vessels globally using Automatic Identification System (AIS) transponder signals and vessel monitoring system data. Machine learning algorithms classify vessel behavior into fishing versus transit modes with approximately 95% accuracy based on speed, heading, and movement patterns. Global Fishing Watch identified over 100,000 suspected fishing vessels that lack AIS transponders, which are often associated with IUU fishing activity. A 2022 study in Nature using Global Fishing Watch data found that over half of global ocean fishing is concentrated in just 1% of ocean area. The platform played a key role in identifying Chinese fishing fleets fishing illegally in North Korean waters during United Nations sanctions. Dark vessel detection uses synthetic aperture radar (SAR) satellite imagery to detect vessels that have turned off their AIS transponders to avoid detection. Global Fishing Watch data has been used by law enforcement agencies in Ecuador, Indonesia, and Peru to identify and prosecute IUU fishing vessels. The organization expanded its monitoring to freshwater fishing and aquaculture sites in 2023, extending its coverage to inland water bodies.
