Document Title: Global Fishing Subsidies

Global government subsidies to the fishing industry total approximately 35.4 billion dollars per year according to a 2019 study published in Marine Policy. Of total subsidies, approximately 22.2 billion dollars are classified as capacity-enhancing, meaning they actively encourage more fishing effort. Fuel subsidies represent the largest single category of fishing subsidies, estimated at 7.4 billion dollars annually. China provides the largest fishing subsidies of any nation, estimated at 7.3 billion dollars per year. The United States provides approximately 2.8 billion dollars annually in fishing subsidies. European Union fishing subsidies total approximately 3.8 billion dollars per year through the European Maritime and Fisheries Fund. Subsidies that reduce the cost of fishing make it economically viable to fish stocks that would otherwise be unprofitable to harvest, directly enabling overfishing. The World Trade Organization has been negotiating a fisheries subsidies agreement since 2001. In June 2022, WTO members agreed to prohibit subsidies for illegal fishing and for fishing overfished stocks, a historic first multilateral agreement on the issue. Implementation of the 2022 WTO agreement requires ratification by two-thirds of WTO members to enter into force.
