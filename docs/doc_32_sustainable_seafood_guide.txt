Document Title: Consumer Sustainable Seafood Guides

Sustainable seafood guides help consumers make purchasing decisions that support well-managed fisheries. The Monterey Bay Aquarium Seafood Watch program rates seafood as Best Choice, Good Alternative, or Avoid based on stock health, fishing method, and management effectiveness. Seafood Watch rates over 300 species and provides region-specific recommendations recognizing that the same species may be sustainably managed in one region and overfished in another. The Marine Conservation Society in the UK publishes the Good Fish Guide using a similar rating framework. Wild-caught Alaskan sockeye salmon, Pacific sardines, and US farmed oysters consistently receive Best Choice ratings. Atlantic bluefin tuna, Chilean sea bass caught by some methods, and imported shrimp from some regions frequently receive Avoid ratings. The Seafood Watch app has been downloaded over 1 million times since its launch. Consumer demand for sustainably sourced seafood has grown; 65% of US consumers say sustainability is important in their seafood purchasing decisions according to a 2022 survey. Major retail chains including Whole Foods, Walmart, and Costco have made public commitments to sourcing certified sustainable seafood. Restaurant chains including McDonald's, Subway, and Long John Silver's have committed to using MSC-certified fish for their fish products. Ecolabels face criticism that they create a false sense of sustainability and that certification costs exclude small-scale fisheries in developing countries.
