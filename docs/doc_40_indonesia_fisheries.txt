Document Title: Indonesia Fisheries Sector

Indonesia is the world's second largest fish producing nation with total production of approximately 23.8 million metric tons in 2021. Indonesia's archipelago encompasses 17,000 islands and 6.4 million square kilometers of ocean, the largest exclusive economic zone in Southeast Asia. Approximately 12.4 million Indonesians work in fisheries and aquaculture. The Coral Triangle, which includes Indonesian waters, contains the highest marine biodiversity on Earth, with over 600 coral species and 2,000 reef fish species. Indonesia lost an estimated 1.4 billion dollars per year to IUU fishing before implementing aggressive enforcement measures in 2014. Former Indonesian Maritime Affairs and Fisheries Minister Susi Pudjiastuti implemented a policy of sinking confiscated foreign fishing vessels, destroying over 500 boats from 2014 to 2019, which dramatically reduced illegal fishing in Indonesian waters. Indonesia imposed a temporary moratorium on foreign fishing vessel licenses in 2014 as part of its IUU crackdown. Fish bomb ing remains a serious problem in eastern Indonesia, particularly around Sulawesi and Maluku, destroying coral reefs. Indonesia is the world's largest exporter of farmed shrimp, producing approximately 550,000 metric tons per year. The Indonesian government's Coastal and Small Island Management Law provides a legal framework for community-based coastal management, though implementation is inconsistent.
