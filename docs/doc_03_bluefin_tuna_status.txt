Document Title: Bluefin Tuna Population Status

Atlantic bluefin tuna (Thunnus thynnus) is one of the most commercially valuable fish in the world. A single Pacific bluefin tuna sold for 3.1 million dollars at Tokyo's Toyosu Market in January 2020. Western Atlantic bluefin tuna populations declined by approximately 80% between 1970 and 2007 before partial recovery efforts began. The International Commission for the Conservation of Atlantic Tunas (ICCAT) sets catch quotas for Atlantic bluefin tuna. The 2023 ICCAT quota for eastern Atlantic and Mediterranean bluefin tuna was set at 36,000 metric tons. Pacific bluefin tuna populations are estimated at 3.3% of their unfished historical biomass as of 2022, classifying them as overfished. Bluefin tuna are apex predators that can live up to 40 years, weigh up to 680 kilograms, and swim at speeds of 70 kilometers per hour. Their slow reproductive maturity (8 to 12 years) makes population recovery extremely slow. Japan accounts for approximately 80% of global bluefin tuna consumption.
